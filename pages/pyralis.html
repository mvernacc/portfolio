Pyralis rocket engine

I lead the development of a 2 kN, ethanol / nitrous oxide, pressure-fed rocket engine with an aerospike nozzle.

Components of the pyralis engine

Pyralis is a liquid-bipropellant rocket engine created by the MIT Rocket Team. I lead the development of Pyralis as Team Predident (2013-14) and Propulsion Lead (2014-15). The engine burns ethanol and nitrous oxide, and produces 2 kN of thrust at a chmaber pressure of 8 MPa. The nozzle is an altitude-compensating aerospike made from machinable alumina ceramic. The combustion chamber is uncooled, and is made from 316 stainless steel insulated with zirconia ceramic.
