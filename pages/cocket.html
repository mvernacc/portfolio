New Shepard Costume

The highlight of the 2015 Aero Astro Halloween party, this costume was build with a rib-and-stressed-skin construction. Andrew and I went from concept to presentation in 22 wild hours.

Inspired by the ... errr... "manly" shape of Bezos's rocket, my friend Andrew Kurtz and I made a New Shepard costume for the Aero Astro department's Halloween party. We won the "Most Aerodynamic" category of the costume contest, and had a lot of laughs.

A rough history of the build:

  • 2 pm - Idea is born
  • 2 pm - 4 pm - Design, sketches and CAD model.
  • 4 pm - 4:30 pm - Determine bill of materials while driving to Home Depot.
  • 4:30 pm - 6 pm - Buy materials.
  • 6 pm - 9 pm - (Break for meetings and dinner).
  • 9 pm - 12 am - Cut ribs on laser cutter.
  • 12 am - 12:30 am - Coffee run
  • 12:30 am - 2:30 am - More laser cutter. Our laser cutter is soooo slow :(
  • 2:30 am - 5:00 am - Assemble and paint ribs.
  • 5:00 am - 8:30 am - Sleep.
  • 8:30 am - Wake up, NOS for breakfast.
  • 9:00 am - 9:30 am - Run to art store to buy canvas for the skin.
  • 9:30 am - 11:30 am - Upholster the canvas skin to the frame.
  • 11:30 am - 12:00pm - Print and apply decals.
  • 12:00 pm - Halloween party
